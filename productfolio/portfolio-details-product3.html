Legno Umbrella Packaging Design

A packaging design for "Legno" umbrellas under the theme "Your Shade, Your Sanctuary." This concept reflects superior protection from both sun and rain, blending the natural essence of wooden handles (Legno) with modern, sophisticated design.

Our process

Key Highlights:
• Packaging Selection
• Design Direction
• Mockup Testing • Layout & Completion

Packaging Selection

Structure: Utilizing a cylindrical tube or an elongated rectangular box tailored to the umbrella’s proportions to prevent deformation and enhance premium value.

Technical Spec: Verifying precise die-cut dimensions based on specific models—such as 3-fold compact or full-length walking umbrellas—prior to the design phase.

Design Direction

Core Concept : Presenting the "Your Shade, Your Sanctuary" identity through graphics that symbolize total protection and personal comfort.

Aesthetic Direction :An Organic Minimalist style using Earth Tones (such as Forest Green or Wood Brown) contrasted with gold or silver accents for a touch of luxury.

Visual Identity: Incorporating fine line art inspired by wood grains and subtle rain patterns to evoke a sense of premium reliability.

Typography: Utilizing classic Serif fonts to convey stability and strength, reflecting the durable structure of the umbrella.

Functional Layout: Systematic organization of essential data, including UV protection ratings (UPF 50+), fabric specifications, and care instructions.


Mockup Testing

Digital 3D Render: Applying designs onto 3D models to ensure graphic continuity and visual balance across all sides of the packaging.

Physical Prototype: Testing print quality on actual materials to calibrate colors and assess the tactile feel of the paper stock.

Tactile Accents: Experimenting with embossing techniques on the logo to elevate the sensory experience and luxury appeal.


Layout & Completion

Final Assembly: Testing structural integrity when the product is fully packaged to ensure safety and durability during transit.

Shelf Presentation: Designing the retail display layout to ensure the brand stands out and communicates its key benefits instantly to customers.

Quality Control: Conducting a final audit of international standard symbols and product information accuracy before moving to mass production.
