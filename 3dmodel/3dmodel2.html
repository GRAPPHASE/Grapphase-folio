3D INTERIOR DESIGN: THE BALANCED LEARNING SPHERE

Concept & Ideology The interior design is driven by the theme of "Harmony in Technology," utilizing the Yin-Yang philosophy to create a balanced learning environment. By integrating ancient symbols of equilibrium with a modern computer lab setting, the design aims to foster a space where digital focus and mental clarity coexist, promoting a more productive educational experience.

Symmetrical Spatial Organization The layout follows a strict "Symmetrical Axis," with two main rows of computer stations flanking a central walkway. This mirrored arrangement not only maximizes the use of the 918 cm x 709 cm area but also reinforces the theme of balance, ensuring that every student has an equal and unobstructed view of the primary presentation screen.

Iconic Wall Art Integration The back wall features a large-scale "Yin-Yang" mural, serving as the room's visual anchor. This choice of iconography transforms a functional computer lab into a "Themed Sphere," where the contrast between black and white elements symbolizes the balance between logical computing (data) and creative thought (humanity).

Technical Orthographic Analysis The presentation includes detailed Top, Front, and Left elevations. These orthographic views are essential for architectural communication, showcasing the ceiling's recessed "Cloud Lighting" design and the precise 340 cm vertical height, proving the design's feasibility and accurate scale for real-world construction.

Modern Material Palette I selected a "Monochrome & Wood" palette to maintain a professional yet warm atmosphere. The grey acoustic wall panels and sleek black computer hardware provide a high-tech feel, while the light wood accents and warm LED lighting prevent the room from feeling sterile, creating a welcoming "Tech-Zen" vibe.

Perspective Depth & Visual Flow The main "Perspective View" highlights the room's depth and visual hierarchy. By positioning the teacher’s station and the large digital display at the far end, the design creates a natural "Focal Point," directing the students' attention toward the source of knowledge while the surrounding bookshelves add a layer of academic functionality.
