3D INTERIOR DESIGN: THE MINIMALIST LEARNING HUB

Concept & Ideology The design core focuses on "Functional Simplicity," aiming to create a distraction-free environment for computer-based learning. By stripping away non-essential decor, the space promotes mental focus and efficient classroom management, ensuring the technology remains the primary tool for education within a clean and modern aesthetic.

Linear Efficient Layout This layout utilizes a "Parallel Linear Configuration" across a 918 cm x 709 cm area. The workstations are organized into two clean, longitudinal rows, providing generous walkway space. This arrangement facilitates easy movement for the instructor and ensures that the 340 cm high room feels spacious and airy rather than cramped.

Natural Light Integration Unlike enclosed labs, this design features large "Horizontal Ribbon Windows." These openings allow for an abundance of natural daylight to flood the room, reducing the reliance on artificial lighting during the day and creating a more comfortable, eye-friendly environment for long periods of screen usage.

Isometric & Orthographic Clarity The presentation provides a clear "Isometric View" along with standard Top, Front, and Left projections. This technical transparency allows for an immediate understanding of the spatial volume, showing how the minimalist furniture fits within the structural dimensions without the need for complex ornamentation.

Warm Wood & Neutral Tones The material palette is strictly "Warm Oak and Soft White." The wooden flooring provides a sense of warmth and durability, contrasting with the neutral walls to create a professional yet inviting atmosphere. This color scheme is intentionally chosen to be timeless and easy to maintain in an institutional setting.

Centralized Instructor Hub A dedicated, oversized instructor desk is positioned at the head of the classroom, equipped with a large digital display. This "Command Center" creates a clear focal point for the room, establishing a logical hierarchy that supports both independent work at the student stations and collaborative group instruction.
